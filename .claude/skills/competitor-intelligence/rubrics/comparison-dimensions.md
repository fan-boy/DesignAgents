# Comparison Dimensions

Evaluate competitors using these dimensions where relevant:

1. Entry point
- How does the user find or start the workflow?

2. Onboarding and setup
- How much effort is required before first value?

3. Core workflow
- What are the main steps?
- How many decisions does the user make?
- Where is friction introduced or reduced?

4. Navigation and IA
- Is the workflow easy to find and understand?
- Are labels and grouping clear?

5. Feedback and system status
- Are loading, success, error, and progress states clear?

6. Error prevention and recovery
- How are mistakes prevented?
- Can users recover easily?

7. Trust and risk communication
- Are consequences explained clearly?
- Are warnings calm, useful, and actionable?

8. Flexibility and efficiency
- Does the flow support both first-time and repeat users?

9. Design quality signals
- clarity
- hierarchy
- consistency
- design-system maturity

10. Differentiation
- What do they do better?
- What should not be copied?
- Where is there white space?
