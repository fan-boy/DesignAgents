## Security Awareness Training (SAT) Platforms

### Adaptive security
**URL:** adaptivesecurity.com
**Relevant for:** Largest SAT market share. The default incumbent in enterprise accounts. Benchmark for training catalog breadth (89+ highlighted titles), phishing simulation volume, and reporting dashboards.
**When to compare:** Displacement deals, enterprise RFPs, any conversation about training catalog depth or phishing simulation scale.
**When NOT to compare:** AI personalization, behavioral risk scoring, or speed of content delivery — KnowBe4's model is volume-based and not adaptive. Comparing here concedes ground we own.

### Proofpoint Security Awareness Training
**URL:** proofpoint.com/us/products/security-awareness-training
**Relevant for:** Strong in email security-adjacent accounts. Often bundled with Proofpoint email gateway. Good benchmark for threat intelligence-informed training.
**When to compare:** Accounts already in the Proofpoint ecosystem. Conversations about threat-correlated training content.
**When NOT to compare:** UX, content quality, or human risk scoring — Proofpoint SAT is not a primary product for them. Don't elevate it.

### Cofense
**URL:** cofense.com
**Relevant for:** Phishing simulation specialist. Deep focus on phishing defense and reporter button integrations. Known for conditioning employees to report suspicious emails.
**When to compare:** Customers specifically evaluating phishing simulation programs or reporter integrations.
**When NOT to compare:** Broad SAT programs, risk scoring, or AI-driven personalization — Cofense doesn't play there.

---

### Curricula (now part of Huntress)
**URL:** huntress.com
**Relevant for:** SMB-focused SAT with narrative/story-based content. Known for humor-driven training videos. Relevant for deals where content style and engagement are differentiators.
**When to compare:** Mid-market deals where content tone and engagement rates come up.
**When NOT to compare:** Enterprise risk management, RBAC complexity, or compliance reporting — not their strength.

---

### Hoxhunt
**URL:** hoxhunt.com
**Relevant for:** Gamified, adaptive phishing training. Strong positioning around behavioral science and habit formation. Closest peer in "adaptive" messaging.
**When to compare:** Any deal where adaptive training or behavioral science framing is in the evaluation criteria.
**When NOT to compare:** Don't over-index on Hoxhunt — their gamification model and our AI personalization model are philosophically different. Avoid direct feature-for-feature comparison.

### Ninjio
**URL:** ninjio.com
**Relevant for:** Animated short-form video training. Known for Hollywood-style production quality and episode format. Good benchmark for content production quality conversations.
**When to compare:** Deals where learner engagement and content quality are primary criteria.
**When NOT to compare:** Risk scoring, simulation depth, or enterprise reporting — Ninjio is a content company, not a platform.

## Human Risk Management Platforms

### Mimecast (Awareness Training)
**URL:** mimecast.com
**Relevant for:** Email security-adjacent, similar to Proofpoint. Acquired Metacompliance. Useful in EMEA-heavy enterprise deals.
**When to compare:** EMEA accounts or Mimecast email security customers evaluating bundled SAT.
**When NOT to compare:** AI capabilities, risk scoring sophistication, or content customization.

---

### CybSafe
**URL:** cybsafe.com
**Relevant for:** Behavioral science-based human risk platform. Strong academic credibility. UK-origin, strong in EMEA financial services. Positions around "reducing human risk" — directly overlapping positioning.
**When to compare:** Deals with explicit behavioral risk language in the RFP, or EMEA financial services accounts.
**When NOT to compare:** US enterprise, simulation depth, or client-branded content production.

---

### SoSafe
**URL:** sosafe-awareness.com
**Relevant for:** European SAT market leader. Behavioral science framing, strong GDPR compliance positioning. Growing US presence.
**When to compare:** EMEA accounts, compliance-heavy verticals (financial, healthcare), deals mentioning GDPR.
**When NOT to compare:** AI personalization depth, spear phishing simulation sophistication, or US market context.

## Social Engineering Defense / Brand Protection

### Doppel
**URL:** doppel.com
**Relevant for:** Brand protection and social engineering defense — monitoring for impersonation, fake domains, executive spoofing. Not a training platform. Relevant for prospects worried about brand-layer threats.
**When to compare:** Executive protection conversations, brand impersonation use cases, or deals where the threat surface extends beyond the employee inbox.
**When NOT to compare:** Training, simulation, risk scoring, or anything platform-related — completely different product category.

## Notes on Competitive Positioning

Avoid head-to-head feature comparisons in these scenarios:

- Against KnowBe4 on catalog size — we win on quality and personalization, not volume.
- Against any incumbent where the buyer is in renewal mode — lead with outcomes and switching cost narrative, not feature lists.
- Against point solutions (Cofense, Doppel) in deals where we're selling the platform story — don't shrink the conversation to their lane.

Dune's defensible differentiation (reference in competitive contexts):

- AI-driven spear phishing personalization at scale
- Human risk scoring tied to behavioral signals, not just training completion
- Client-branded SAT module factory (speed to deployment)
- Remediation agent tying risk scores to automated intervention
- Enterprise-grade content production (multilingual, white-label quality)
